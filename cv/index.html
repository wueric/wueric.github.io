Publications

  • 2022
    Maximum a posteriori natural scene reconstruction from retinal ganglion cells with deep denoiser priors
    NeurIPS

    Nonlinear inverse problem for decoding static natural images from retinal neural spikes trains, combining interpretable generalized linear neural encoding models with deep denoising neural network image priors for MAP decoding. Demonstrated state-of-the-art decoded image fidelity (on ImageNet) using electrophysiologically-recorded spike trains from large populations of retinal ganglion cells (700+ cells), while using orders-of-magnitude fewer model parameters than competing methods.

  • 2024
    Fixational eye movements enhance the precision of visual information transmitted by the primate retina
    Nature Communications

    Simultaneous decoding of natural movies and eye movements from retinal neural spike trains using nonlinear inverse problem formulation (combining generalized linear neural encoding models, deep denoising neural network image priors, and expectation maximization for approximate MAP decoding). First experimental demonstration of blind motion deblurring and super-resolution imaging from electrophysiologically-recorded spike trains of retinal ganglion cells, showing that the primate visual system can exploit fine eye movments to improve fidelity of the visual signal.

  • Decomposition of retinal ganglion cell electrical images for cell type and functional inference
    Journal of Neural Engineering

    Non-convex optimization algorithm to estimate and isolate axonal, dendritic, and somatic signal components from extracellularly-recorded spike voltage templates of retinal ganglion cells. Featurizations produced by this algorithm enabled significant gains in accuracy over state-of-the-art for predicting receptive fields and retinal ganglion cell types from voltage signals alone, with applications for retinal prosthetic devices that restore vision.

  • Autonomous sweat extraction and analysis applied to cystic fibrosis and glucose monitoring using a fully integrated wearable platform
    PNAS

    Integrated electronic system for wearable sweat stimulation and sweat metabolite and electrolyte chemical sensing, applied to cystic fibrosis diagnosis and real-time glucose monitoring. I designed the sensor interface and sweat stimulation electronics, wrote all of the embedded firmware, and participated in experimental evaluation for the system.
